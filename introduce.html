The Magic Behind the Game



What is Game of Life?

The Game of Life is a cellular automation, invented by a mathmatician John Horton Conway

It starts with living cells spread on the canvas, and they evolve according to predefined rules.

Rules

(1) Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if caused by under-population.

(2) Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.

(3) Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overcrowding.

(4) Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

How is our game of life different?

More funs !!!

We created some new features including Pacman, Mines, Snakes, and Infected Cells.

Infected Cells

Infected cells are like a regular cell but of different colors and slightly different rules. They cannot appear on their own and will only be initially generated by the user. If a living cell has at least one infected cell neighbour, then it may become an infected cell neighbor by some predefined chance. If it has two infected neighbors, the chance increases. The same also applies if there are three infected neighbors.

Snake

The snake is generated randomly. All the cells will be dead after it passes through them.

If a snake hits a mine, the mine will explode as well.

If the snake hits a wall, it will stop and become dead.

The user has three options of how to control the snakes and can be discovered by experimenting with them.

Pacman

Pacman will eat all the mines and kill all the cells when it passes by.

Mines

The mines are generated randommly. If a living cell or snake touches the mine, it will explode and kill all cells in a certain radius of its center.
